Filipino Pork Sinigang


Description

Filipino pork sinigang is a delightful and savory soup known for its tangy tamarind broth, tender pork chunks, and a medley of vegetables. With its harmonious blend of sourness, umami,
and natural sweetness, this comforting dish holds a special place in the hearts of Filipinos, bringing families and friends together around the dining table.

Ingredients

  • Pork: Approximately 1.5 to 2 pounds (700 to 900 grams) of pork belly, ribs, or shoulder, cut into chunks.
  • Tamarind: About 2 to 3 tablespoons of tamarind paste or 8 to 10 pieces of fresh tamarind pods.
  • Water or broth: 10 to 12 cups (2.5 to 3 liters) of water or pork/beef broth.
  • Radish: 2 to 3 medium-sized radishes, sliced.
  • Eggplant: 2 medium-sized eggplants, sliced.
  • String beans (Green beans): A handful or about 15 to 20 pieces, trimmed and cut into 2-inch lengths.
  • Tomato: 2 medium-sized tomatoes, quartered.
  • Onions: 1 large onion, sliced.
  • Garlic: 4 to 6 cloves, minced.
  • Fish sauce (or salt): 3 to 4 tablespoons, adjust to taste.
  • Pepper: 1/2 to 1 teaspoon, adjust to taste.
  • Green chili (optional): 2 to 3 pieces, sliced (adjust according to spice preference).
  • Water spinach (kangkong) or spinach leaves (optional): A handful or about 2 to 3 cups, washed and trimmed.

    • These quantities are approximate and can be adjusted based on personal preferences and taste.

Steps

  1. In a large pot, bring the water or broth to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Once the water is boiling, add the pork chunks and let it simmer for about 30 minutes or until the pork becomes tender.
  3. While the pork is cooking, prepare the tamarind by soaking the tamarind pods in warm water for a few minutes.
    Extract the tamarind juice and discard the solids. If using tamarind paste, simply dissolve it in a cup of warm water and strain to remove any seeds or pulp.
  4. Once the pork is tender, add the tamarind juice to the pot, followed by the radishes, eggplants, string beans, tomatoes, onions, and minced garlic.
  5. Season the sinigang with fish sauce (or salt) and pepper, adjusting the amount to suit your taste preference.
  6. For those who prefer a spicier sinigang, add the sliced green chili at this point.
  7. Let the sinigang simmer for another 10 to 15 minutes or until the vegetables are cooked but still slightly crisp.
  8. Lastly, add the water spinach or spinach leaves (if using) and cook for an additional 2 minutes until the greens wilt.
  9. Taste the sinigang and adjust the seasoning if necessary.
  10. Serve the hot and flavorful Filipino pork sinigang in bowls, with steamed rice on the side. Enjoy this delicious and comforting soup with your family and friends!
